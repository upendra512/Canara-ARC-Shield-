# RBI Cybersecurity Framework for Banks

## Section 5 - Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
Banks must implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all administrative access to critical servers and customer-facing digital banking portals. MFA must include at least two independent factors: knowledge (password), possession (OTP/Token), or inherence (Biometrics) to protect customer sessions.

## Section 8 - Data Encryption Standards
All customer personal data and transaction data must be encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher protocols, and at rest using AES-256 or higher encryption algorithms. Encryption keys must be rotated at least once every 12 months to prevent unauthorized disclosure.

## Section 14 - Incident Reporting Timelines
Any cyber security incident, security breach, or unauthorized access to bank databases must be reported to the RBI and CERT-In within 6 hours of detection or identification. A detailed forensic investigation report must be submitted within 14 days.

## Section 20 - Security Operations Center (SOC)
Banks must operate a 24x7 Security Operations Center (SOC) to monitor, detect, and respond to potential threats in real-time. The SOC must perform automated log analysis of all critical infrastructure, firewall logs, and active directory activities.
