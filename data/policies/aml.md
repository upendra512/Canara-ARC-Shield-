# RBI Master Direction - Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Standards

## Section 22 - Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)
Banks shall monitor customer transactions for unusual patterns or sudden spikes in volume. Any suspicious transaction must be reported to the Financial Intelligence Unit - India (FIU-IND) within 7 days of arriving at a conclusion that the transaction is indeed suspicious or relates to proceeds of crime.

## Section 24 - Cash Transaction Reporting (CTR)
All cash transactions exceeding INR 10 Lakh or its equivalent in foreign currency, or a series of integrally connected cash transactions that aggregate to more than INR 10 Lakh in a single calendar month, must be reported to FIU-IND via Cash Transaction Reports (CTR) by the 15th of the succeeding month.

## Section 30 - Watchlist and Sanctions Screening
Banks must run real-time sanctions screening of all customer names, beneficial owners, and cross-border transaction counterparties against the consolidated United Nations Security Council (UNSC) sanctions list, OFAC list, and local watchlists before onboarding and processing transactions.

## Section 35 - Transaction Monitoring and Alerts
All transaction alerts generated by the automated AML monitoring system must be investigated and closed within 30 days of alert generation. Alerts flagged as high-risk require immediate escalation to the Principal Officer for further investigation.
