# KYC Master Directions — Version 2 (Amended: July 2026)
# Domain: KYC
# Source: RBI/DBOD/2026/KYC-MD-002

## Paragraph 38 - Customer Due Diligence for Individuals
Banks shall carry out Customer Due Diligence by obtaining the following documents from individual customers at the time of account opening. A certified copy of any one document notified by the Central Government as proof of identity along with one document as proof of address is mandatory. Permanent Account Number or Form 60 as defined in Income Tax Rules shall be obtained compulsorily. Aadhaar number shall be obtained from customers who voluntarily provide it. No account shall be opened in anonymous or fictitious names. The identity verification must be completed within 12 hours of account opening initiation.

## Paragraph 45 - Periodic Updation of KYC
Regulated entities shall ensure periodic updation of KYC records for all customers. The frequency of updation shall be at least once in two years for high risk customers, at least once in eight years for medium risk customers, and at least once in ten years for low risk customers. Banks shall obtain fresh KYC documents from customers when their risk profile changes from lower to higher category. Failure to complete periodic KYC updation shall result in partial freezing of the account whereby credits shall not be permitted. Only debit transactions essential for the livelihood of the customer shall be allowed after partial freeze. A notice period of thirty days shall be given to the customer before imposing partial freeze.

## Paragraph 56 - Video Customer Identification Process
The Video Customer Identification Process may be carried out by a bank official using live video. The process shall include a live photograph of the customer, capture of the PAN card, and an OTP sent to the customer's mobile number registered with Aadhaar. The bank official conducting the V-CIP must be a trained employee. All V-CIP sessions must be recorded and stored for a minimum period of three years. The video quality must be sufficient to identify the customer clearly. Customers whose Aadhaar is not seeded with a mobile number shall not be eligible for V-CIP.

## Paragraph 65 - Central KYC Records Registry
Banks shall register customers on the Central KYC Records Registry for all accounts opened on or after January 1, 2017. A unique fourteen digit KYC Identifier shall be issued to each customer registered on the CKYC Registry. Banks downloading KYC records of existing customers from the CKYC Registry are exempt from fresh physical verification, provided the match score is at least 95%. Physical re-verification is not mandatory for these downloaded CKYC profiles, representing a substantial shift to streamline operations.

## Paragraph 89 - Record Keeping and Reporting Obligations
All records relating to the identity of customers and their transactions shall be maintained for a minimum period of five years after the business relationship has ended. Transaction records exceeding the prescribed threshold of ten lakh rupees shall be reported to the Financial Intelligence Unit within seven working days. Suspicious transaction reports shall be filed within seven working days of the transaction being identified as suspicious. Banks shall not tip off the customer regarding the filing of any suspicious transaction report. All KYC records must be made available to the authorities within forty eight hours of request.

## Paragraph 95 - Digital Signature and KYC Audits
Regulated entities shall conduct annual independent audits of their digital onboarding and KYC systems. The audit report must be submitted to the Department of Supervision, Reserve Bank of India, within thirty days of closure of the financial year.
