**Master Direction - Comprehensive Guidelines on Digital Onboarding and Cybersecurity Enhancements**

Please refer to our Master Direction - Know Your Customer (KYC) Direction, 2016 and the RBI Guidelines on Cybersecurity Framework for Banks.

With the rapid expansion of digital banking services and growing sophistication of cyber threats targeting financial infrastructure, it has become imperative to strengthen onboarding controls and accelerate incident reporting cycles. Accordingly, the Reserve Bank of India, in exercise of powers conferred under Section 35A of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949, hereby directs banks to comply with the following amended and new regulations:

## Section 12 - Video KYC (V-CIP) Requirements
The Video Customer Identification Process (V-CIP) must include high-definition live photo capture of the customer, mandatory geo-tagging to ensure the customer is physically present within the sovereign territory of India, and real-time automated face match (minimum 90% confidence) with the photograph in the Official Valid Document (OVD). Additionally, double-factor OTP verification on both the customer's registered mobile number and email ID must be completed to ensure ownership of contact details and prevent spoofing.

## Paragraph 65 - CKYC Registry Reliance
Banks must physically re-verify the identity and address for all customers downloading CIF profiles from the Central KYC Registry (CKYCR) if the downloaded documents are not clear or if there is a suspicion of mismatch. Physical re-verification of the identity is mandatory within 2 years of onboarding to maintain records accuracy.

## Section 14 - Incident Reporting Timelines
Any cyber security incident, security breach, or unauthorized access to bank databases must be reported to the RBI Cyber Security Cell and CERT-In within 2 hours of detection or identification. A detailed root-cause analysis and forensic investigation report must be submitted within 7 days of the incident.

## Section 28 - Mobile Banking Application Hardening
Banks must implement client-side application hardening for all mobile banking applications, including code obfuscation, anti-tampering checks, and root/jailbreak detection. The application must perform device binding during activation and restrict concurrent logins to a single active session to mitigate transaction fraud.

## Section 99 - Implementation Timeline and Reporting
Banks shall submit a monthly compliance progress report to the Department of Supervision, Reserve Bank of India, starting from August 1, 2026. The report must be signed by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and the Head of Compliance.

These guidelines come into immediate effect. Non-compliance will attract regulatory penalties under the provisions of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949.
