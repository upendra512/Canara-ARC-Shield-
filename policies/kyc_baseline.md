# KYC Master Directions — Version 1 (Baseline: January 2024)
# Domain: KYC
# Source: RBI/DBOD/2024/KYC-MD-001

## Paragraph 38
Banks shall carry out Customer Due Diligence by obtaining the following documents from individual customers at the time of account opening. A certified copy of any one document notified by the Central Government as proof of identity along with one document as proof of address is mandatory. Permanent Account Number or Form 60 as defined in Income Tax Rules shall be obtained compulsorily. Aadhaar number shall be obtained from customers who voluntarily provide it. No account shall be opened in anonymous or fictitious names. The identity verification must be completed within 24 hours of account opening initiation.

## Paragraph 45
Regulated entities shall ensure periodic updation of KYC records for all customers. The frequency of updation shall be at least once in two years for high risk customers, at least once in eight years for medium risk customers, and at least once in ten years for low risk customers. Banks shall obtain fresh KYC documents from customers when their risk profile changes from lower to higher category. Failure to complete periodic KYC updation shall result in partial freezing of the account whereby credits shall not be permitted. Only debit transactions essential for the livelihood of the customer shall be allowed after partial freeze. A notice period of thirty days shall be given to the customer before imposing partial freeze.

## Paragraph 56
The Video Customer Identification Process may be carried out by a bank official using live video. The process shall include a live photograph of the customer, capture of the PAN card, and an OTP sent to the customer's mobile number registered with Aadhaar. The bank official conducting the V-CIP must be a trained employee. All V-CIP sessions must be recorded and stored for a minimum period of six months. The video quality must be sufficient to identify the customer clearly. Customers whose Aadhaar is not seeded with a mobile number shall not be eligible for V-CIP.

## Paragraph 65
Banks shall register customers on the Central KYC Records Registry for all accounts opened on or after January 1, 2017. A unique fourteen digit KYC Identifier shall be issued to each customer registered on the CKYC Registry. Banks downloading KYC records of existing customers from the CKYC Registry shall carry out fresh physical verification of identity and address documents at the time of download. Physical re-verification of original documents is mandatory regardless of the availability of digital records in the Registry. No exemption from physical re-verification shall be granted to any category of customer including those with V-CIP history.

## Paragraph 72
Banks shall apply enhanced due diligence measures for all customers classified as high risk. High risk customers include Politically Exposed Persons and their family members and close associates. Enhanced due diligence shall include obtaining source of funds documentation, senior management approval for account opening, and enhanced ongoing monitoring at quarterly intervals. Banks shall not open accounts for foreign nationals from high risk jurisdictions without prior approval from the Compliance department head. All high risk account opening decisions must be documented with specific reasons recorded.

## Paragraph 89
All records relating to the identity of customers and their transactions shall be maintained for a minimum period of five years after the business relationship has ended. Transaction records exceeding the prescribed threshold of ten lakh rupees shall be reported to the Financial Intelligence Unit within seven working days. Suspicious transaction reports shall be filed within seven working days of the transaction being identified as suspicious. Banks shall not tip off the customer regarding the filing of any suspicious transaction report. All KYC records must be made available to the authorities within forty eight hours of request.
