# KYC Master Directions — Version 1 (Baseline: January 2024)
# Domain: KYC
# Source: RBI/DBOD/2024/KYC-MD-001

## Paragraph 38
Banks shall carry out Customer Due Diligence at the time of account opening. A certified copy of proof of identity and address is mandatory. Aadhaar number shall be obtained voluntarily. The identity verification must be completed within 24 hours of initiation.

## Paragraph 45
Regulated entities shall ensure periodic updation of KYC records. The frequency shall be once in two years for high risk, eight years for medium risk, and ten years for low risk customers. Failure to complete updation shall result in partial freezing of the account.

## Paragraph 56
The Video Customer Identification Process (V-CIP) may be carried out by a trained bank official. All V-CIP sessions must be recorded and stored for a minimum period of six months. Customers without Aadhaar seeding are not eligible.

## Paragraph 65
Banks shall register customers on the Central KYC Records Registry (CKYCR) within 3 days. Banks downloading KYC records of existing customers shall carry out fresh physical verification. No exemption from physical re-verification shall be granted.

## Paragraph 72
Banks shall apply enhanced due diligence (EDD) measures for high risk customers, including Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs). EDD shall include senior management approval and quarterly ongoing monitoring.

## Paragraph 89
All records relating to customer identity and transactions shall be maintained for five years after the business relationship has ended. Suspicious transaction reports shall be filed to FIU within seven working days.
