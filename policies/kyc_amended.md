# KYC Master Directions — Version 2 (Amended: July 2026)
# Domain: KYC
# Source: RBI/DBOD/2026/KYC-MD-002

## Paragraph 38
 A certified copy of proof of identity and address is mandatory. Aadhaar number shall be obtained voluntarily. The identity verification must be completed within 12 hours of initiation.

## Paragraph 45
Regulated entities shall ensure periodic updation of KYC records. The frequency shall be once in two years for high risk, eight years for medium risk, and ten years for low risk customers. Failure to complete updation shall result in partial freezing of the account.

## Paragraph 56
The Video Customer Identification Process (V-CIP) may be carried out by a trained bank official. All V-CIP sessions must be recorded and stored for a minimum period of three years. Customers without Aadhaar seeding are not eligible.

## Paragraph 65
Banks shall register customers on the Central KYC Records Registry (CKYCR) within 3 days. Banks downloading KYC records of existing customers are exempt from fresh physical verification, provided the match score is at least 95%.

## Paragraph 89
All records relating to customer identity and transactions shall be maintained for five years after the business relationship has ended. Suspicious transaction reports shall be filed to FIU within seven working days.

## Paragraph 95
Regulated entities shall conduct annual independent audits of their digital onboarding and KYC systems, submitting the report to the Reserve Bank of India within thirty days of the financial year closure.
